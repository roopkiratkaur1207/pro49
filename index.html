Camel

Intresting facts about camels

  1. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  2. When a camel finally does find water, he can drink up to 40 gallons in one go.
  3. Camels can travel at up to 40 miles per hour – the same as a racehorse!

Chameleon

Intresting facts about chameleons

  1. Chameleon eyes have a 360-degree arc of vision and can see two directions at once.
  2. Chameleons have ballistic tongues that are 1.5-2 times the length of their body.
  3. Chameleons can’t hear much.

Pigeon

Intresting facts about pigeons

  1. Pigeons acn understand space and time.
  2. They can easily get back to their nest from a distance of 1300 miles.
  3. They can be trained to be art snobs and to distinguish written words.

Teddy Bear

Intresting facts about Teddy Bears

  1. The first toy bear is believed to be made by German toy maker Margarete Steiff in 1902 that was a jointed, stuffed one.
  2. American multi-billionaire investor Paul Greenwood owned the largest collection of teddy bears.
  3. The term “bear hug” meaning a tight embrace was first used in 1846, almost 60 years before teddy bear was “born”.

Panda

Intresting facts about Pandas

  1. Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  2. An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!
  3. Pandas have 6 toes to grasp bamboo.

Flying Cicada

Intresting facts about Cicadas

  1. Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs.
  2. The periodical cicadas of North America spend 13 or 17 years underground.
  3. They improve lawns by digging tunnels that allow air into the soil. When they die, their rotting bodies put nitrogen into the soil.
